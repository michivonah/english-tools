Tenses

Present simple

Present simple is used to describe events in the present.

Person Form
I, You, We, They Infinitve
He, She, It Infinitve + s

Examples

  • I cook some noodles.
  • You learn english!
  • He buys new clothes.

Present continous

Present continous is used to describe events that have already started and are still going on.

Person Form
I, You, He, She, It, We, They to be + verb-ing

Examples

  • I am shopping for my birthday.
  • You are studying for the exam next week.
  • He is cooking a meal for today.

Wishes

We have two types of wish sentences.

  • I wish for something I can change
  • I wish for something someone other can change

Take a look at the following table:

Type of the wish Form Example
I can change it I wish + had + verb 3* I wish I had learned to swim
Someone else can change it I wish + they + present simple I wish they would buy a new oven

*Verb 3 means the form of verb ending with -ed except for the irregular verbs
